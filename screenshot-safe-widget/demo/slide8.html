DeepSeek-R1-Zero — Reasoning emerges from incentives, not instructions
NOVEL
What they proved
First open model to show advanced reasoning — self-reflection, backtracking — can emerge from pure RL with zero supervised CoT data. Starting from DeepSeek-V3-Base, GRPO with rule-based accuracy reward + format reward (no neural reward model, no human demonstrations) raised AIME 2024 from 15.6% → 77.9% pass@1 and 86.7% cons@64.
The unique finding
"Wait, wait. Wait. That's an aha moment I can flag here…" — R1-Zero self-correction, Table 3. Not designed in. Incentivized out.

DeepSeek-R1 — Multi-stage pipeline stabilizes what pure RL broke
PIPELINE
What they proved
R1-Zero suffered language mixing, poor readability, repetition. R1's 4-stage pipeline (cold-start SFT → RL → rejection SFT → RL) fixed all three while raising final scores to 79.8% AIME, 97.3% MATH500, 2029 Codeforces Elo. Then distilled to six open checkpoints: 1.5B, 7B, 8B, 14B, 32B, 70B (MIT license).
The unique finding
Cold-start SFT before RL prevents instability. Language consistency reward suppresses mixing. ~800k SFT samples (600k reasoning + 200k general) restore general capability that pure RL erodes — without sacrificing reasoning scores.

Sky-T1 (NovaSky) — Data mixture and trace format are first-class variables
$450
What they proved
Competitive reasoning cloned from QwQ-32B-Preview via SFT on 17k samples. 19 wall-clock hours on 8×H100 (~152 GPU-hours) for $450 at Lambda Cloud. First fully open (data + code + weights) dual-domain model. AIME 43.3%, on par with o1-preview (40.0%).
The unique finding
Adding code data naively dropped AIME 43.3% → 36.7%. GPT-4o-mini reformatting lifted code parse accuracy from ~25% → 90%+. Trace format and data mixture, not just volume or cost, determine outcome.
NovaSky Team (2025) novasky-ai.github.io/posts/sky-t1
Bespoke Labs — The bottleneck is the verifier, not the volume
KEY FIX
What they proved
Replacing Sky-T1's regex/sympy verifier with GPT-4o-mini answer checking lifted correct-trace retention from 25% → 73%. With 17k samples and DeepSeek-R1 as teacher, Bespoke-Stratos reached 63.3% AIME, 93.0% MATH500 — 47× fewer examples than DeepSeek's own distill checkpoint (800k).
The unique finding
Sky-T1 was silently discarding ~75% of correct training examples due to a faulty parser. The distillation bottleneck is verification accuracy, not dataset size. Also showed 7B distillation works with high-quality data — contradicting Sky-T1's "little gain below 32B" observation, attributed to higher trace quality.
