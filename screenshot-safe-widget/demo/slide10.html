Three things this work changed about reasoning AI
What the field now knows that it didn't before 2025
1
Democratisation is real — frontier-level reasoning no longer requires frontier-scale compute
Sky-T1 reached o1-preview parity on AIME (43.3 vs 40.0), MATH500 (82.4 vs 81.4), and LiveCodeBench-Med (56.8 vs 54.9) for $450 — 19 wall-clock hours on 8×H100 (~152 GPU-hours). DeepSeek released six distilled checkpoints (1.5B–70B) MIT-licensed with explicit distillation permission. The capital barrier became a data-curation problem.

2
The bottleneck shifted — from inventing reasoning to selecting and preserving high-quality traces
Once DeepSeek-R1 exists as an open teacher, the hard problem is no longer "can we afford RL at scale?" It is "can we identify and preserve the teacher's best reasoning traces?"

3
Practical rule: without frontier RL budget, distill — don't reinvent
DeepSeek's controlled experiment compared Qwen2.5-32B trained with 10k+ RL steps vs SFT distillation from R1. RL from scratch lost on every benchmark.
"Distilling more powerful models into smaller ones yields excellent results… whereas smaller models relying on large-scale RL may not even achieve the performance of distillation."
