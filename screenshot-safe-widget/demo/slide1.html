Why reasoning was a frontier-labs-only capability
— and why that changed in early 2025
Three barriers that locked reasoning inside frontier labs
Compute
RL training at scale
Long chain-of-thought traces require massive GPU hours to generate and train on. Only OpenAI, Google could afford it.
Access
Proprietary weights & traces
o1's reasoning traces and weights were never released. The community had no open teacher to learn from.
Knowledge
No open recipe
How to design rewards, curate CoT data, and stabilise RL for reasoning was undocumented outside closed labs.
What broke each barrier — Jan 2025
DeepSeek releases R1-Zero & R1 — open weights, full paper (arXiv:2501.12948). 79.8% AIME 2024 pass@1, matching o1. Compute barrier broken by a non-US lab.
NovaSky releases Sky-T1-32B-Preview — competitive reasoning for $450 (19 wall-clock hours on 8×H100, ~152 GPU-hours), full data + code open-sourced. 43.3% AIME 2024, on par with o1-preview (40.0%).
Bespoke Labs releases Bespoke-Stratos-32B — same pipeline, stronger teacher. 63.3% AIME 2024 on just 17k examples, 47× fewer than DeepSeek's own distill checkpoint.
