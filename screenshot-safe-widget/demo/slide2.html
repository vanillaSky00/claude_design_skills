Slide 2 · Knowledge Distillation: The Field Today
From model compression to reasoning transfer
Where the field stands and what it still struggles with
Three eras of distillation
Era 1 — 2015–2020
Compression
Hinton et al. (2015): small model mimics large model's soft output probabilities. Goal: reduce size, preserve accuracy. No reasoning transfer.
Era 2 — 2021–2023
Task Transfer
Alpaca, WizardLM: distill GPT-4 instruction-following via SFT on synthetic data. Teacher output as training label. Reasoning not the focus.
Era 3 — 2024–2025
Reasoning Transfer ← Now
Sky-T1, R1-Distill, Bespoke-Stratos: distill long chain-of-thought reasoning traces. Goal: clone emergent reasoning behavior, not just answers.
What made reasoning transfer possible
Long CoT traces as training signal
Not just the answer — the full internal monologue. Reasoning chains can span thousands to tens of thousands of tokens, exposing the student to how the teacher thinks, not just what it concludes.
Open strong teachers (2025)
DeepSeek-R1 released MIT-licensed with explicit distillation permission. First time a near-frontier reasoning model was legally and practically available as a teacher.
What the field still struggles with
Hard limit
Teacher ceiling: SFT-distilled students cannot exceed what the teacher can demonstrate. ~13% of hard problems are unsolvable even with N=64 teacher samples. HEAL, arXiv:2603.10359 (2026)
Bias
GRPO length bias: RL training inflates response length, especially for wrong answers. Longer ≠ better — overthinking and underthinking are both documented failure modes. arXiv:2503.20783 · arXiv:2505.00127
Domain gap
Verifiable domains only: gains concentrate in math, code, STEM. Open-ended tasks, function calling, multi-turn conversation remain weak. DeepSeek-R1, arXiv:2501.12948, §4
Nuanced
Hallucination — pipeline-dependent: distillation-only or RL without cold-start can worsen hallucination vs backbone; full SFT+RL pipelines can reduce it. arXiv:2505.23646 · arXiv:2510.22977
