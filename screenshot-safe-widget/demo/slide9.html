Teacher ceiling — unreachable via SFT alone
SFT distillation is "a pure imitation process" — the student cannot solve problems beyond the teacher's abilities. Even sampling N=64 times, the teacher still fails on ~13% of hard problems, silently excluding them from training data and biasing the student toward easier examples.

Bias GRPO length bias
GRPO normalizes loss by token count, under-penalizing long wrong answers. Models drift toward verbose incorrect responses. "Longer thinking" ≠ better thinking — reward and length can both rise while accuracy stalls.
Dr. GRPO, arXiv:2503.20783 · Between Underthinking and Overthinking, arXiv:2505.00127
Nuanced Hallucination — pipeline-dependent
Distillation-only or RL without cold-start can worsen hallucination vs the backbone. Full cold-start SFT + RL pipelines (as in R1) can reduce it. Reasoning gains and factuality can move in opposite directions depending on pipeline design.
Are Reasoning Models More Prone to Hallucination?, arXiv:2505.23646 · The Reasoning Trap, arXiv:2510.22977
Gap Domain limitation
Gains concentrate in verifiable domains (math, code, STEM). DeepSeek-R1 is inferior to DeepSeek-V3 on function calling, structured JSON, role-playing, and multi-turn conversations. Non-math agentic tasks remain weak. Prompt sensitivity: few-shot degrades performance.
DeepSeek-AI, arXiv:2501.12948, §4 limitations
Gap Mimicry vs mastery
SFT "collapses rich reasoning structure into flat token prediction" — students copy the form, not the teacher's search policy. Small distilled students "mechanically replicate patterns without internalising reasoning logic," producing repetition with no final answer.
RLKD, arXiv:2505.16142 · Marco-o1 v2, arXiv:2503.01461 · Hán Dān Xué Bù (Mimicry) or Qīng Chū Yú Lán (Mastery)? arXiv:2601.05019
