Slide 3 · Distillation Methodology Landscape
SFT, Chain-of-Thought Traces, and RL
Three tools — very different tradeoffs
Method 1
Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)
How: train student on teacher's (input, output) pairs
Standard next-token prediction. No reward model. No search.
Cheap Stable Teacher-bounded
Used by: Sky-T1, Bespoke-Stratos, R1-Distill series
Method 2
Chain-of-Thought Traces
How: teacher generates long internal reasoning paths; student learns the full trace
Thousands to tens of thousands of tokens per problem. Exposes how to think, not just the answer.
Transfers reasoning style Quality-sensitive
The key ingredient in all three labs
Method 3
Reinforcement Learning (RL)
How: model improves via reward signal (GRPO/PPO). No teacher demonstrations needed
R1-Zero uses rule-based accuracy reward + format reward — no neural reward model. Can discover novel strategies.
Can exceed teacher Expensive Length bias
Used by: R1-Zero (pure), R1 (hybrid SFT+RL)
SFT-distillation vs RL-from-scratch — same Qwen2.5-32B base
Model Method AIME 2024 MATH500 LiveCode Med
R1-Zero-Qwen-32B (RL only, 10k+ steps) Pure RL 47.0% 91.6% 40.2%
R1-Distill-Qwen-32B (SFT distillation) SFT 72.6% 94.3% 57.2%
At 32B scale, SFT distillation crushes large-scale RL from scratch on every benchmark. DeepSeek: "distilling more powerful models into smaller ones yields excellent results… whereas smaller models relying on large-scale RL require enormous computational power and may not even achieve the performance of distillation."
